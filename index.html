Course Overview

Modern computing has transitioned from monolithic data centers to hyper-connected heterogeneous ecosystems. This course, NT2204, provides a comprehensive graduate-level exploration of Distributed Systems architecture, focusing on the core theoretical foundations and practical engineering paradigms that power today's internet-scale platforms.

We investigate the evolution of systems through the lens of Cloud-native design, Serverless FaaS, and Distributed Intelligence. Key topics include Dataflow execution models, consensus-driven storage (e.g., Raft/Spanner), and the orchestration of AI models across edge-cloud continuums. By balancing rigorous paper reading with a heavy capstone project, students learn to architect systems that are scalable, fault-tolerant, and privacy-preserving.
