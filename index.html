NT2204: Advanced Distributed Computing Systems

The landscape of modern computing has shifted from isolated clusters to a hyper-connected, heterogeneous ecosystem. This course provides a comprehensive exploration of the advanced theoretical and practical dimensions of Distributed Systems. Our focus extends beyond classical distributed algorithms to the engineering of contemporary cloud-native infrastructures, serverless computing, and decentralized AI.

Students will investigate the fundamental tradeoffs in distributed data processing, exploring Dataflow execution models and complex scheduling via MapReduce-style paradigms. A significant portion of the curriculum is dedicated to the intersection of distributed systems and machine learning, specifically focusing on Distributed Intelligence and Adaptive Service Orchestration. By the end of this course, students will be equipped to architect systems that are not only scalable and fault-tolerant but also preserve privacy and maintain security in adversarial environments.
